# Theory

## Introduction

The Kitaev chain is a one-dimensional model of a p-wave topological superconductor proposed by Alexei Kitaev in 2001.

Despite its simplicity, the model exhibits non-trivial topological phases and supports Majorana zero modes localized at the boundaries of the system.

The Kitaev chain has become one of the most important theoretical models in condensed matter physics and topological quantum computation.

---

## Majorana Fermions

A Majorana fermion is a particle that is its own antiparticle.

In condensed matter systems, Majorana quasiparticles can emerge as collective excitations in topological superconductors.

Majorana zero modes possess non-Abelian exchange statistics and are considered promising candidates for fault-tolerant quantum computation.

---

## Kitaev Chain Hamiltonian

The Kitaev chain Hamiltonian is

$$
H =
-\mu \sum_i c_i^\dagger c_i
-
t \sum_i
(c_i^\dagger c_{i+1}+h.c.)
+
\Delta \sum_i
(c_i c_{i+1}+h.c.)
$$

where:

- μ is the chemical potential
- t is the hopping amplitude
- Δ is the superconducting pairing strength

---

## Topological Phases

The Kitaev chain exhibits two distinct phases.

### Topological Phase

$$
|\mu| < 2t
$$

Characteristics:

- Majorana zero modes
- Edge localization
- Non-trivial topology

### Trivial Phase

$$
|\mu| > 2t
$$

Characteristics:

- No Majorana modes
- Conventional superconducting behavior

---

## Topological Phase Transition

The phase boundary occurs at

$$
|\mu| = 2t
$$

At this point, the bulk energy gap closes and the topological invariant changes.

---

## Topological Quantum Computation

Majorana zero modes are of significant interest because information encoded in spatially separated Majorana states is naturally protected against many local sources of decoherence.

This property makes topological superconductors a promising platform for fault-tolerant quantum computation.
